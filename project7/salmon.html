Lemon Garlic Salmon

Sheet-pan salmon with roasted vegetables, ready in about 20–25 minutes.

Why this recipe works

This recipe uses one baking sheet, basic pantry seasonings, and a short cooking time. It works well for dorms or apartments with a small oven and does not require advanced cooking skills.

Ingredients

  • 1 salmon fillet (about 4–6 ounces)
  • 1–2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, green beans, or frozen veg mix)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or other cooking oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (or 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder)
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: lemon slices, dried herbs (like parsley or Italian seasoning)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (about 200°C). Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment if you want easier cleanup.
  2. Place the vegetables on one side of the pan. Drizzle with about half the oil, sprinkle with salt and pepper, and toss to coat.
  3. Place the salmon fillet on the other side of the pan. Brush or drizzle with the remaining oil.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, lemon juice, a pinch of salt, and pepper. Spoon this mixture over the salmon. Add lemon slices on top if you have them.
  5. Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and the vegetables are tender. Thicker pieces of salmon may need an extra 3–5 minutes.
  6. Let cool slightly and serve the salmon with the roasted vegetables on the side.

Ingredient swaps and tips

  • No fresh garlic? Use garlic powder instead.
  • No fresh vegetables? Use frozen veggies; just add 2–3 extra minutes in the oven.
  • No oven? Cook the salmon and vegetables in a covered pan on the stovetop over medium heat with a bit of oil and water.
  • Gluten-free: This recipe is naturally gluten-free; double-check bottled sauces if you add any.
  • Dairy-free: The recipe is dairy-free as written.
