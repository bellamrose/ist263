Overnight Oats

A no-cook, grab-and-go breakfast you can prep the night before.

Why this recipe works

Overnight oats take less than 5 minutes to prepare and keep well for 2–3 days in the fridge. They’re filling, customizable, and inexpensive — perfect for busy mornings.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup milk (any kind)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (optional but makes it creamier)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional, thickens the mixture)
  • 1–2 teaspoons honey or maple syrup
  • Toppings: berries, peanut butter, bananas, nuts, cinnamon

Steps

  1. Add oats, milk, yogurt, chia seeds, and sweetener to a jar or container.
  2. Stir well until everything is combined.
  3. Cover the container and refrigerate at least 4 hours, or overnight for best texture.
  4. In the morning, stir again and add your favorite toppings.
  5. If the mixture is too thick, add a splash of milk before eating.

Ingredient swaps and tips

  • Dairy-free: Use almond, oat, or soy milk and a plant-based yogurt.
  • Gluten-free: Use certified gluten-free oats.
  • High protein: Add a spoon of protein powder or Greek yogurt.
  • No yogurt? Just add a little extra milk.
  • Budget tip: Buy oats in bulk — they’re one of the cheapest nutritious foods.
