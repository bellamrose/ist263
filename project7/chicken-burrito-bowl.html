Chicken Burrito Bowl

Meal-prep friendly bowls with rice, beans, veggies, and your favorite toppings.

Why this recipe works

This recipe uses budget-friendly staples like rice and beans. You can cook once and assemble different bowls for several days, which saves time and money during a busy week.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded or diced (rotisserie or leftover works)
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (frozen or canned, drained)
  • 1 cup chopped vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, peppers, or whatever you have)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (optional)
  • Salsa, hot sauce, or dressing of your choice
  • Salt, pepper, and optional taco seasoning

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to the package directions. Let it cool slightly before using in bowls.
  2. Warm the chicken, beans, and corn in the microwave or on the stovetop until heated through.
  3. Season the chicken with a pinch of salt, pepper, and taco seasoning if you have it.
  4. To build each bowl, add a base of rice, then top with chicken, beans, corn, and chopped vegetables.
  5. Add shredded cheese, salsa, hot sauce, or your favorite dressing on top.
  6. Serve right away or store components separately in the fridge and assemble bowls during the week.

Ingredient swaps and tips

  • Vegetarian: Skip the chicken and add extra beans, tofu, or saut√©ed veggies.
  • Dairy-free: Leave out the cheese or use a plant-based alternative.
  • Gluten-free: This recipe is naturally gluten-free when you use gluten-free seasonings.
  • No stove? Use microwave rice pouches and pre-cooked chicken or beans.
  • Meal prep: Store rice, chicken, and beans in separate containers and assemble bowls fresh for better texture.
